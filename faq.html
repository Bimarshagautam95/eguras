How can we help you?

Studying abroad provides access to better education, global exposure, and the chance to experience different cultures. It can enhance career opportunities and personal growth through high-quality academic programs and networking.

Popular study destinations for Nepali students include the USA, UK, Australia, Canada and Japan. Each country offers unique advantages depending on your course preferences, budget, and future plans.

Requirements vary by country and institution but generally include:
  • Valid passport
  • Educational certificates (transcripts, degrees)
  • Proof of English proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL, PTE)
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Letters of Recommendation (LOR)
  • Financial documents
  • Visa application

Prepare strong documentation, including a clear study plan, proof of financial stability, and a convincing Statement of Purpose. Showing ties to Nepal and explaining your career goals after studying can also help.

The cost depends on the country and university. Expenses include tuition fees (which can range from $10,000 to $40,000 per year), accommodation, food, health insurance, and travel. Scholarships and financial aid can help reduce costs.

Yes, many countries and universities offer scholarships based on academic merit, financial need, or specific criteria (such as field of study). Common scholarships include:
  • Chevening Scholarship (UK)
  • Australia Awards
  • DAAD (Germany)
  • Fulbright (USA)
  • University-specific scholarships

English proficiency tests such as IELTS, TOEFL, or PTE are commonly required. Some programs may also require entrance exams like the SAT, GRE, GMAT, or LSAT depending on the course and level of study.

Yes, many countries allow international students to work part-time during their studies (typically 20 hours per week during term time). However, work regulations and post-study work opportunities vary by country.

Consider factors such as:
  • The university’s global ranking
  • Availability of your desired course
  • Scholarships and financial aid options
  • Location, living expenses, and student support services
  • Alumni network and career services
